Duck a l'orange

Description

Duck a l'orange is one of those classic dishes that somehow became a clich√©, and people stopped making it for fear of looking un-cool, which is too bad, since it's really good. This is traditionally done with a whole roasted duck, but by using duck breasts we get pretty much the same results in a lot less time.

Ingridients

  • 2 duck breast halves
  • salt to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons orange liqueur (such as Grand Marnier)
  • 1 tablespoon sherry vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Seville orange marmalade, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons grated orange zest
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon reserved duck fat
  • 1 teaspoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter
